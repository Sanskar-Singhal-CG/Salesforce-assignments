Part 1: Conceptual Questions - Answers

1. What is a Flow in Salesforce and why is it used?
A Flow is a tool in Salesforce that lets you automate tasks without writing code. 
You just drag and drop elements to build a process, like collecting info from a user or updating records automatically.
2. Differentiate between Screen Flow and Autolaunched Flow.
A Screen Flow shows screens to the user so they can enter or see information while the flow is running, kind of like filling out a form.
An Autolaunched Flow runs quietly in the background, triggered by something like a record being created or updated.

3. What are variables in Flows and how are they used?
Variables are like containers that hold information temporarily while the flow is running, 
for example a name, email, or number. 
You use them to store data collected from the user or from a record, and then use that stored value later in the flow.

4. Explain the role of the Decision element in a Flow.
The Decision element is a "if then" step.
It checks a condition, for example if the condition is greater than a certain value,
it then sends the flow to different paths depending on whether the condition is true or false.

5. What are the limitations of using Flows compared to Apex?
Flows are great for simple to medium automation, but they can get messy and hard to manage for very complex logic. 
Apex code is more powerful and flexible and it can handle complicated business rules better.
